About Me

Hello! I'm Ryan Byers, a passionate Computer Science graduate with a focus on web development. Right now, I am currently pursuing a full time position as a full-stack web developer! If you are hiring, view my Contact section for details on how to get in touch with me.

My interest in programming began in my first programming course in college back in 2019. Still undecided on my major, I was like many other freshman in college: feeling quite lost regarding what I wanted to pursue as a career. Even then, I'd always had a love for computers, but at the time that interest was derived from my love of video games, so I wasn't entirely positive that a Computer Science degree would translate my love into fulfilling work. So, with my being unsure with what to do, I simply decided to get my associates in General Studies degree during my time at Wor-Wic University in Salisbury, Maryland.

During my time at Wor-Wic, I decided to enroll for electives that would give me exposure to Computer Science with hope I would find some inspiration. Luckily, once I took my first programming course, Introduction to Python, I did. After learning Python at Wor-Wic, I fell in love with programming, and as such, my idea of the perfect career for me post-graduation instantly switched to that of a software or a web developer.

From here, my path forward began to become clearer to me. I was decided on finishing my Associates at Wor-Wic, then pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Science elsewhere. After graduation from Wor-Wic in 2020, my family and I moved from Maryland to Missouri in order to both live closer to my older brother, and to also return to the town in which I grew up in during 2010-2016: Parkville. Once settled in, I put into motion my decision to achieve my Bachelor's Degree, and from 2021-2025, I worked part-time at hotel located in downtown Kansas City, Loews Kansas City, as a bellman whilst I pursued my Bachelor's Degree full-time at The University of Missouri-Kansas City (UMKC).

During my time at UMKC, I began work on a few projects in order to display my programming ability to potential employers. My first project centers around the creation of a 3-D renderer under the following limitations: it is built in C++ and uses only the Simple Direct Media Library 2 (SDL2). The challenges I faced in the planning stage of the project stemmed from my lack of knowledge at the time. I didn't know how or where I should start with the project. I decided to research what other people with a similar project as I did during this stage, and tried my best to replicate their strategies in the hopes of getting the project off the ground. I started small, calling a SDL2 method to draw a single, almost invisible pixel on the screen. From here, I wrote a simple method to draw a straight line of pixels, then from those lines came a square, then finally, a (depthless) cube. From here it felt my project finally had legs, so progress picked up dramatically. I changed the user's POV to include depth as part of the rendering process via projecting the user's POV to the world space, and rendering based on that. Finally, I added functionality to cull vertices that are off the screen or occluded by other objects within the sim, and even vertices being blocked by an object's own face. Now I am working on the ability for the renderer to take models exported from Blender (in a particular format, most likely .obj). My portfolio page! The planning stage of this project was short and smooth. This was surely due to the various pitfalls and mistakes I made during the development of my first project. Additionally, I was becoming more and more intimate with Github and Git CLI, making large mistakes less impactful, and small ones incredibly easy to undo. With commits helping me to understand what I did and when I did it, I was able to start and stop development on this project during the school semester without having to take unnecessary time to catch myself up. My most recent project was my Senior Software Engineering Capstone course. This course entailed the creation of a full-stack web application made within teams desginated by the professor. Where before my struggles came from lack of technical knowledge, now my struggles come from my lack of ability to work cohesively within a team for a task such as this. Many of the problems with the project stemmed from the lack of a chosen team leader. We took time to organize the tasks we needed to comeplete using an online software called Jira. Jira allowed us to break our work into small segements that we eventually broke down further into sprints, which allowed us to adhere to the agile development cycle, which was encouraged by the professor. Some team members lacked motivation, and assumed there would be others to pick up the slack. With all of this, it is fair to say I was surprised by all of the problems my team and I encountered during the course of this project, as I thought my previous experience would be enough to carry me and the rest of my team through, but I was wrong. Ultimately, these problems persisted for some time whilst our team made menial incremental improvements and additions to the site.
